Fighting Online Scams Through Awareness

Helping victims, researchers, and scambaiters stay informed.

About This Project

ScamWatch is an independent awareness project dedicated to documenting scam operations, educating the public, and assisting scam baiters in identifying fraudulent tactics.



This project tracks known scam techniques including phishing websites, fake technical support services, and social engineering attacks.

Exposing Fraudulent Operations

Recently Exposed Scammers

Fake Tech Support Call Centers

Large call centers impersonating companies like Microsoft and Apple to trick victims into paying fake repair fees.

Phishing Infrastructure

Networks hosting thousands of fake login pages designed to steal passwords and financial data.

Gift Card Fraud Rings

Groups targeting elderly victims and demanding payment through gift cards.

Common Scam Websites

Fake Antivirus Support

Often appear through pop-ups claiming your computer is infected.

Fake Banking Login Pages

Designed to steal login credentials through phishing.

Fake Cryptocurrency Exchanges

Used in investment scams and pig-butchering schemes.

Official Legitimate Services

Official Company Support Pages

Always use verified websites when contacting support.

Government Fraud Reporting

Report scams through official reporting portals.

Cybersecurity Organizations

Trusted sources that track scam campaigns worldwide.

Programs Often Used by Scammers

Remote Access Software

Scammers often trick victims into installing remote desktop software to gain control of their computer.

Phishing Kits

Pre-built packages that generate fake login pages for banks and email services.

Voice Over IP Systems

Allows scammers to call victims worldwide while hiding their real location.
