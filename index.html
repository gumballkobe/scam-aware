Common Scam Types

Phishing

Fake emails and websites designed to steal passwords and banking information.

Tech Support Scams

Fake virus alerts convince victims to call scammers posing as Microsoft or Apple.

Fake Online Stores

Fraud websites selling products that never arrive.

Inside a Scam Operation

Many scams originate from organized call centers where workers follow scripts to impersonate banks, government agencies, and tech companies. These operations often use VoIP phone systems, phishing websites, and remote desktop software to gain control of victims computers.

Tools Used by Scammers

Remote Access Software

Programs like AnyDesk and TeamViewer are abused to control victim computers.

Phishing Kits

Prebuilt login pages that capture usernames and passwords.

VoIP Systems

Used by scam call centers to spoof phone numbers.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Never give remote access to strangers.
  • Check website URLs carefully.
  • Use two-factor authentication.
  • Report suspicious calls or emails.
